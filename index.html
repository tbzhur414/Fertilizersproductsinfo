Fertilizer Products in Pakistan

Explore production, capacity, demand, regional use, crop suitability, and application timing

Report by Hur Abbas Rana

NP and NPK Fertilizers (Demand: ~0.34M MT/year)

Summary: NP and NPK fertilizers provide balanced nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium for a wide range of crops, enhancing root development, fruit quality, and yield. Key products include Engro NP, Zarkhaiz SOP/MOP, and Fatima NP (Sarsabz, Pakarab). Total production in Pakistan is approximately 405,000 MT/year, meeting a significant portion of the 340,000 MT/year demand, with the remainder imported, particularly SOP and MOP components. These fertilizers are critical for high-yield crops like wheat, rice, and fruits, especially in Punjab and Sindh.

Engro NP Product

Brand: Engro NP

Composition: 18% N, 18% P2O5

Production

Produced at Engro Fertilizers’ plants in Dharki, Ghotki, Sindh (100,000 MT/year), and Port Qasim, Karachi (100,000 MT/year). Total NP capacity: 200,000 MT/year.

Uses

Crops: Wheat, rice, cotton, sugarcane, vegetables, fruits.
Regions & Suitability: Punjab (Faisalabad, Multan; fertile alluvial soils, ideal for wheat, cotton), Sindh (Hyderabad, Mirpurkhas; suitable for rice, sugarcane).
Months: Rabi (Nov–Jan) for wheat; Kharif (Jun–Aug) for rice, cotton, sugarcane.
Application Timing: Applied basally at sowing, mixed into soil; phosphorus supplemented at first irrigation if missed at sowing.

Importance

Ensures root and shoot growth; increases yields by 10–15% in cereals.

Urea Fertilizers (46% N, Demand: ~6.2M MT/year)

Summary: Urea, the most widely used fertilizer in Pakistan, supplies high nitrogen content for vigorous crop growth. Key brands include FFC Sona Urea, Engro Urea, Fatima Urea, and Agritech Urea. Pakistan produces approximately 6,110,000 MT/year, nearly meeting the 6,200,000 MT/year demand, with minor imports. It is essential for major crops like wheat, rice, and sugarcane, primarily in Punjab and Sindh, supporting 70% of the nation’s nitrogen needs.

FFC Urea Products

Brands: Sona Urea Prilled, Sona Urea Granular, Urea Zinc Coated, Urea Neem Coated, Urea Tara

Production

Produced at Fauji Fertilizer Company’s plants in Goth Machhi (RYK), Sadiqabad, Punjab (1,660,000 MT/year), and Mirpur Mathelo, Sindh (830,000 MT/year). Total capacity: 2,490,000 MT/year.

Uses

Crops: Rice, wheat, maize, sugarcane, cotton, vegetables, orchards.
Regions & Suitability: Punjab (Faisalabad, Multan; loamy soils for wheat, cotton), Sindh (Hyderabad, Mirpurkhas; clayey soils for rice, sugarcane), Khyber Pakhtunkhwa (Mardan; suitable for maize, orchards).
Months: Rabi (Nov–Jan) for wheat; Kharif (Jun–Aug) for rice, maize, cotton, sugarcane.
Application Timing: Applied basally (50%) at sowing, top-dressed (50%) at first/second irrigation; for rice, basal in mud, top-dressed at panicle initiation.

Importance

70% of Pakistan’s N consumption; improves yield by 10–20%, enhances sustainability.

DAP Fertilizers (18% N, 46% P2O5, Demand: ~1.2M MT/year)

Summary: Diammonium Phosphate (DAP) fertilizers provide nitrogen and high phosphorus content, addressing widespread phosphorus deficiency in 80% of Pakistani soils. Key brands include FFC Sona DAP, Engro DAP, and Fatima DAP (Pakarab, Babar Sher). Pakistan produces 900,000 MT/year, with 300,000 MT/year imported to meet the 1,200,000 MT/year demand. DAP is vital for crops like wheat, rice, and pulses, especially in Punjab, Sindh, and Balochistan.
